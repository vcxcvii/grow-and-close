# ICP Sharpener (B2B SaaS)

Turn "we sell to B2B companies that need X" into an ICP a marketer or SDR can
act on without asking follow-up questions.

## What you need before starting

Gather (ask for whatever is missing):

1. **Product**: what it does, in the user's own words. One paragraph max.
2. **Evidence**: 5–15 real accounts — best customers, worst customers, recent
   churns, recent closed-lost. Names optional; descriptions required.
3. **Deal mechanics**: typical ACV, sales cycle length, who signed, who blocked.

If the user only has a website URL, fetch it, infer a draft, and label every
inference as an assumption to validate.

## Workflow

### Step 1: Split "customer" into three roles

For each good-fit account, identify separately — these are rarely the same
person and conflating them is the #1 ICP error:

- **User**: who touches the product weekly
- **Buyer**: who signed / owns the budget line
- **Champion**: who dragged the deal through internally

### Step 2: Find the pattern in the evidence, not the aspiration

Compare best vs. worst accounts across: company size, funding stage, tech
stack, team structure, motion (PLG/sales-led), and the situation right before
they bought. The ICP is what the best accounts share that the worst lack —
not who the founder wishes would buy.

### Step 3: Name the buying trigger

For each strong account, answer: *what changed that made them start looking?*
(New leader hired, funding round, tool ripped out, compliance deadline, growth
stalled.) An ICP without triggers produces outbound with no reason to reply.

### Step 4: Write the disqualifiers

List 3–5 hard disqualifiers (too small, wrong stack, no budget owner, needs
on-prem). A segment you can't say no to isn't a segment.

### Step 5: Output the ICP one-pager

Produce exactly this structure:

```
## ICP: <segment name — specific, e.g. "Series A–B PLG SaaS, 20–80 FTE, first marketing hire just landed">

**Firmographics**: size, stage, geo, stack
**User / Buyer / Champion**: title + what each cares about (one line each)
**Buying triggers**: 2–4 observable events
**Disqualifiers**: 3–5 hard nos
**Where they live**: communities, events, newsletters, who they follow
**Message angle**: the one sentence that names their situation better than they can
**Confidence**: which parts are evidence-backed vs. assumed — and the fastest way to test the assumptions
```

If the evidence supports two distinct segments, write two one-pagers and
recommend which to lead with (score on: revenue evidence, reachability, sales
cycle speed). Never output more than three — more than three ICPs is zero ICPs.

## Quality bar

- Every claim traces to an account in the evidence or is flagged as assumption.
- A stranger could build a 200-account list from the firmographics alone.
- The message angle names a *situation*, not a benefit ("your first marketing
  hire just inherited a backlog," not "save time and grow faster").
